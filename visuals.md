## Visual Style: Dark Editorial / Candlelit Confidence

The aesthetic reference point is a Michelin guide formatted for midnight. Not playful. Not the typical food-app red and white. Think: the moment you sit down at a dimly lit restaurant and feel at ease before you've even ordered. That's Kaena's job — to radiate the confidence of a friend with impeccable taste saying *"trust me, we're going here."*

Dark, warm backgrounds. High-contrast cream type. A single amber accent that reads like candlelight. Every screen should feel like the decision is already made.

---

## Color Palette

| Role | Hex | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Background | `#0E0A07` | Near-black with a warm undertone, not cold |
| Surface | `#1C1310` | Warm dark espresso, for cards and sheets |
| Accent | `#F0A030` | Amber / candlelight gold — the only color with energy |
| Primary text | `#F4ECDA` | Warm cream |
| Muted text | `#87776E` | Warm stone, for secondary info |

Five colors max. The amber does all the heavy lifting. Everything else is warmth and restraint.

---

## Typeface Pairing

**Display — Cormorant Garamond**
High-contrast editorial serif. The kind of font you'd find engraved on a fine dining menu. Used for hero headlines like *"Here's where you're eating tonight."* and the wordmark itself. Free on Google Fonts.

**UI / Body — DM Sans**
Humanist geometric sans-serif. Warm, clean, reads perfectly at small mobile sizes. Has subtle personality without being distracting. Intentionally not Inter — that font has zero character. Also free on Google Fonts.

The tension between these two is the whole point: editorial authority from Cormorant, approachable clarity from DM Sans. One says *Michelin*, the other says *open right now.*
